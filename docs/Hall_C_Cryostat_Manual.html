Cooling-Down procedure

Leak check DU \(\to\) Hall-C

  • Please leak check the DU (Dilution Unit) flanges every time that you open them to air.
    • Typically, between two consecutive runs, you are forced to unplug the condenser KF-10 bellow and the still ISO-K100, both on top of the cryostat.
    • The DU leak check should be performed at room temperature.
      The recommended leak rate should be \(< 10^{-9} \ \rm{mbar*l/s}\).

Rinse the DU

  • To achieve an ideal leak rate, you can rinse the DU circulation path with high-purity N2 (Nitrogen).
    • Because after years of using, a large amount of He (Helium) remains inside the pipes.
    • The high-purity nitrogen could come from the LN2 (Liquid Nitrogen) dewar.
  1. Firstly, clean around v10 and v11.
  2. Then put 100 mbar of N2 in the still, through
    • \(\rm{VENT1 \to v11 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\) .
  3. And then use ROOTS pump \(\to\) AUX (auxiliary) pump to pump it.
    • This process should then be repeated 3 times.
    • The leak rate should seem to be improved.

IVC pumping

  • Inside IVC (Inner Vacuum Chamber), there are extremely delicate crystals that should be either flushed with high-purity N2 or be pumped.
    • Because the surface of the crystal is extremely deliquescent, nitrogen can help absorb moisture and pumping can remove it.
  • To pump IVC, you can use the turbo \(\to\) leak detector or the turbo \(\to\) AUX.
    • Whichever you choose, make sure you have a certain vacuum level before turning on the turbo.
    • It is always good to use Pman (the red pressure sensor which we looted from CUORE) to monitor the pressure.
      • Actually in Hall-C, we use a T to allow us to monitor both IVC and OVC.

DU pumping

  1. Before operation, the IVC is being pumped with turbo and leak detector.
  2. Close the valve on the IVC and turn off the turbo.
  3. While waiting for the turbo to decelerate, pump the cryostat from the back end with the BYPASSofSTILL.
  4. After that, open the valve of the condenser line, and pump from the front end.
  5. Use the BYPASS A and B to clean the ROOTS pump.
  6. Open the QSB valve in front of the ROOTS pump, allowing the cryostat to be pumped by the ROOTS pump.

    Always check the situation of the QSB valve after shutting off the power to the panel.

  7. With BYPASSofSTILL, pump the condenser.

The path of reference

  • \(\rm{v12 + v13 \to ROTARY \to G4}\)
    • The right side of valves 12 and 13 are pumped by the ROTARY pump, and the output is accumulated at G4;
  • \(\rm{still \to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)
    • The cryostat is pumped from the still, the QSB valve is opened, and use the ROOTS pump, also the BYPASS B and A are open, and finally reach the auxiliary pump;
  • \(\rm{condenser \to v_{condenser} \to v3 \to v2 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\) \(\rm{\to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)
    • Use BYPASSofSTILL to pump the condenser line of cryostat, and also use the ROOTS pump;
  • \(\rm{trap \to v1A \to v2 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\) \(\rm{\to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)
    • We opened the 1A valve and we pump the liquid nitrogen trap at the same time.

Cold finger inserting

  • Insert the LHe (Liquid Helium) transferring line (cold finger) from the liquefier into the cryostat MB (Main Bath).
    • When inserting, be sure to have two O-rings inside the ferrule.
      • After inserting the cold fingers, then secure the first O-ring in the groove.
    • In addition, during the insertion process, there will be two layers of baffles that will hinder you.
      • Solve it by rotating instead of using force.

Routine leak check

Leak check between MB and OVC

  1. After the turbo was ready, open the valve of the OVC and, using the LD (Leak Detector), checked the He leak rate of the OVC.
  2. Wait for the vacuum in OVC to almost reach its limit.
  3. Using the 1KPOT pump, pump the MB above 400 mbar.
  4. Then fill the MB with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of LD).
  5. Close the valve of the OVC.

Vacuum baseline of MB

  1. Use both AUX pump and 1KPOT pump simultaneously to pump MB.

    The baseline of vacuum could reach \(7 \times 10^{-2} \ \rm{mbar}\), read from P4.

Natural leak rate of MB

  1. Keep the MB isolated and check the MB leak rate with reference to pressure changes.

    It was 0.3 mbar/min., while v21 is open.

Leak check between MB and IVC

  1. Open the valve of the IVC, pump the MB.
  2. Then fill the MB with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of LD).

Leak check between 1KPOT and IVC

  1. Using the 1KPOT pump, pump 1KPOT.
  2. Then fill the 1KPOT with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of LD).

Electronics measurements

  • Before moving the IVC and after closing the MB, an electronic communication measurement is highly recommended.
  • At the black pins, you can measure every channel you need, but at the Fischer interface, you will not be able to measure the EF interface.
  • In addition to each channel itself, also measure the grounding of individual pins.

DU capillary flow test

  • Please check that the He flows regularly through the condenser line of the DU.
  • In order to make this test, pressurize the still volume (read at G2) with He gas from a bottle.

    Make sure that the condenser BYPASS is closed (G3 should not increase while G2 increases).

  • But perform compatible measurements, G2 should always arrive precisely at the black tick just above 1000 mbar.
  • Then open v3 (v2 & v1A & v1 are closed) and measure the pressure increase over time on G3 (take into account the offset).

    A suggestion is to measure the increase over a longer period than a minute (like 5 or 10 minutes).

    • The DU capillary flow test must be performed at room temperature, at 77K and at 4K.
    • The reference capillary flow in 2020-2021 are:
      • 3.5 mbar/min @ room T
      • 18 ~ 19 mbar/min IVC @ 77 K, MC @ 110 ~ 100 K (-19 umol/s on \(^{3}\rm{He}\) flow reader)
      • 60 ~ 70 mbar/min IVC @ 5.1 ~ 4.7 K, MC @31.5~24.6 K (-90 ~ -80 umol/s on \(^{3}\rm{He}\) flow reader)
  1. Stop the pumping of cryostat, turn off the ROOTS pump.
    • (Closed the condenser BYPASS.)
  2. Fill the cryostat with He-2 from still, through
    • \(\rm{v11 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\).
  3. Pump the gas from the condenser.
    • That means you start the counter flow of the circulation path of DU.
  4. Close v1 and suspend the counter flow of the circulation path for 5 min.
    • That means you perform the capillary test.
  5. After that restore the counter flow of the circulation path.

Leak check DU \(\to\) IVC

  • Since is a good practice to counter flow the capillary during a cooldown, this test comes for free in each moment of the cooldown when you are pumping the IVC with the LD.
    • The leak check DU \(\to\) IVC must be performed at room temperature, at 77K and at 4K and comes for free monitoring the IVC leak rate while making the capillary flow test (for which you have to pressurize the still with 1 bar of He).
  • Reaching a good leak rate in IVC is not easy if you use He as exchange gas also from 300K down to 77K.

    A reference value of leak rate in IVC is less than few \(10^{-7} \ \rm{mbar*l/s}\) while cooling down.

Flow test 1Kpot needle valves

  1. Fill 1KPOT with He-1, and test the flow rate of low-impedance and high-impedance needle valves respectively.
  2. Then filled the MB and 1KPOT with He-1.

IVC filling with N2

  1. Redo a capillary test.
  2. Stop pumping of cryostat.
  3. Provide He-2 from still to cryostat using BYPASSofSTILL.
  4. Use v1 to pump from the condenser.
  5. Fill the 1KPOT with He-1.
  6. Filled the MB with N2.
  7. Keep the IVC with 20 mbar of N2.

MB filling with LN2

  1. Raise the cold finger a bit.
  2. Open the valve between the liquefier and the MB, and perform a counter flow from the MB to the getter.
    • When the pressure of MB is found to be oscillating, which means that the liquid nitrogen begins to transfer.
    • Control the pressure of MB around 890 mbar.
  3. During the transferring of LN2, perform two capillary tests by closing v1.
  4. When the overpressure is not enough, stop the counter flow of the liquefier.

Re-transferring of LN2

  1. Insert the LN2 transfer line and start the re-transferring of LN2 into the Dewar.
  2. Put some overpressure nitrogen in the MB for re-transferring the LN2 back to the dewar.
    • At first, you can use N2 to squeeze, then you can switch to He-1.

Liquefier preparation

  • We connected the cold box to the turbo, using the “OVC” and “IVC” pipes.
  • We started pumping the cold box, by the turbo and the external pump.
  • After the pumping for one night, we turned off the turbo and stopped the coldbox pumping.
  • After the turbo is stopped, we attached the IVC to the turbo.
  • We turned on GM heads and JT, and using the cooldown bypass.
  • Periodically measure the working point.

Tests at LN temperature

  • Repeat the contact check.
  • Repeat the capillary test.
  • Repeat the leak check (if you put nitrogen as exchange gas).
    • After the transferring is complete, we use the 1KPOT pump to pump the MB.
    • We connected the IVC to the leak detector.
  • Repeat the needle valve check.
    • Re-pump MB in preparation for flow test for both precision needle valves.
  • We closed the IVC, stopped the turbo and turned off the leak detector.

MB filling with LHe

  • start to transfer without waiting for the LHe flame of the boa. The MB and the 1KPot should be in communication and both to recovery. In the IVC there should be some He gas to be used as exchange gas. Moreover, the final part of this helium transfer is different from the refill one because it has to be interrupted at precise conditions.

Cleaning the LN2 trap

  • Various impurity gases may be absorbed in the cold trap, which can be removed by heating.
  1. Isolate the LN2 trap.
  2. Pump the LN2 trap from v4A.
  3. Heat the LN2 trap up.

Maintenance

1KPOT pump

  • The 1KPOT pump currently used in Hall-C is for pumping 1KPOT, and occasionally as well for MB.
  • Its model number is D40B and it has gas ballast.
  • Here you can find its manual.

Oil changing

  • Types of oil that can be used:
    • Oil N 62;
    • Oil HE-200.
  • The oil grades N 62 and HE-200 are interchangeable.
    • Caution: Only use the kind of oil specified by Leybold. Alternative types of oil are specified upon request.
  • On initial start-up, after prolonged idle periods or after an oil change, the specified ultimate pressure cannot be attained until the oil is degassed. This can be done by running the pump for approximately 30 minutes with the intake line closed and the gas ballast valve (7/5) open.
