Cooling-Down procedure

Leak check DU -> Hall-C

Please leak check the DU (Dilution Unit) flanges every time that you open them to air.
Typically, between two consecutive runs, you are forced to unplug the condenser KF-10 bellow and the still ISO-K100, both on top of the cryostat.

  • The DU leak check should be performed at room temperature, the recommended leak rate should be less than \(10^{-9} \ \rm{mbar*l/s}\).
  • we decided to flush the DU circulation path with high-purity nitrogen.
    • Because after years of use, a large amount of helium remains inside the pipes.
  • First we cleaned around valves 10 and 11.
  • Then we put 100 mbar of nitrogen in the still, and then use the ROOTS pump plus the auxiliary pump to pump it.
  • This process was then repeated 3 additional times.
  • The leak rate seemed to have improved.

IVC pumping

  • The IVC is being pumped with turbo and leak detector.

DU pumping

Before operation, the IVC is being pumped with turbo and leak detector.

  • We closed the valve on the IVC and turned off the turbo.
  • While waiting for the turbo to decelerate, we pump the cryostat from the back end with the BYPASSofSTILL.
  • After that, we opened the valve of the condenser line, and pumped from the front end.
  • We used the BYPASS A and B to clean up the ROOTS pump.
  • We opened the QSB valve in front of the ROOTS pump, allowing the cryostat to be pumped by the ROOTS pump.

The path of reference:

  • \(\rm{v12 + v13 \to ROTARY \to G4}\)

The right side of valves 12 and 13 are pumped by the ROTARY pump, and the output is accumulated at G4;

  • \(\rm{still \to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)

The cryostat is pumped from the still, the QSB valve is opened, and use the ROOTS pump, also the BYPASS B and A are open, and finally reach the auxiliary pump;

  • \(\rm{condenser \to v_{condenser} \to v3 \to v2 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\) \(\rm{\to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)

Use BYPASSofSTILL to pump the condenser line of cryostat, and also use the ROOTS pump;

  • \(\rm{trap \to v1A \to v2 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\) \(\rm{\to QSB \to ROOTS \to BYPASS\,B \to BYPASS\,A \to AUX}\)

We opened the 1A valve and we pump the liquid nitrogen trap at the same time.

Cold finger inserting

  • We inserted the liquid Helium delivery line from the liquefier into the cryostat MB.

Routine leak check

Leak check between MB and OVC

  • After the turbo was ready, we opened the valve of the OVC and, using the LD (Leak Detector), checked the He (Helium) leaking rate of the OVC.
  • We waited for the OVC vacuum to almost reach its limit.
  • Using the 1KPOT pump, we pumped the MB and then filled the MB with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of the leak detector).
  • Closed the valve of the OVC.

Natural leak rate of MB

  • We kept the MB isolated and checked the MB leak rate with reference to pressure changes.

It was 16 mbar/min.

Vacuum baseline of MB

  • At the same time, the auxiliary pump and 1KPOT pump were used to pump MB, and the level of baseline can be achieved reached 1.0 * 10^-1 mbar, read from P4.

Leak check between MB and IVC

  • Opened the valve of the IVC, pumped the MB, and then filled the MB with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of the leak detector).

Leak check between 1KPOT and IVC

  • Using a 1KPOT pump, pumped 1KPOT and then filled the 1KPOT with He (gradually, per 200 mbar, while checking the leak rate of the leak detector).

DU capillary flow test

Please check that the He flows regularly through the condenser line of the DU.

  • In order to make this test, pressurize the still volume (read at G2) with He gas (5.0 is enough) from a bottle and through
    • \(\rm{v11 \to v6 \to v7 \to BYPASSofSTILL}\).

Make sure that the condenser BYPASS is closed (G3 should not increase while G2 increases).

  • But perform compatible measurements, G2 should always arrive precisely at the black tick just above 1000 mbar.
  • Then open v3 (v2-v1A-v1 closed) and measure the pressure increase over time on G3 (take into account the offset).

A suggestion is to measure the increase over a longer period than a minute (like 5 or 10 minutes).

  • The DU capillary flow test must be performed at room temperature, at 77K and at 4K. The reference capillary flow in 2020-2021 are:
    • 3.5 mbar/min @ room T
    • 18 ~ 19 mbar/min IVC @ 77 K, MC @ 110 ~ 100 K (-19 umol/s on \(^{3}\rm{He}\) flow reader)
    • 60 ~ 70 mbar/min IVC @ 5.1 ~ 4.7 K, MC @31.5~24.6 K (-90 ~ -80 umol/s on \(^{3}\rm{He}\) flow reader)
  • We stopped the pumping of cryostat, turned off the ROOTS pump.
  • Closed the condenser BYPASS.
  • We filled the cryostat with He-2 from still, pumped gas from the condenser. That means we started the counter flow of the circulation path.
  • We closed v1 and we suspended the counter flow of the mixture circulation path, which means we performed the capillary test.
  • After that we restored the counter flow of the circulation path.

Leak check DU -> IVC

  • The leak check DU -> IVC must be performed at room temperature, at 77K and at 4K and comes for free monitoring the IVC leak rate while making the capillary flow test (for which you have to pressurize the still with 1 bar of He).

Since is a good practice to counter flow the capillary during a cooldown, this test comes for free in each moment of the cooldown when you are pumping the IVC with the leak detector.
Reaching a good leak rate in IVC is not easy if you use He as exchange gas also from 300K down to 77K.

A reference value of leak rate in IVC is less than few \(10^{-7} \ \rm{mbar*l/s}\) while cooling down.

Flow test 1Kpot needle valves

  • Filled 1KPOT with He-1, and test the flow rate of low-impedance and high-impedance needle valves respectively.
  • Then filled the MB and 1KPOT with He-1.

IVC filling with \(\rm{N}_{2}\)

  • Redo a capillary test
  • We stopped pumping of cryostat. We changed the path of the gas to provide He-2 from still to cryostat using BYPASSofSTILL. We also used valve 1 to pump from the condenser.
  • We filled the 1KPOT with He-1;
    • filled the MB with nitrogen;
    • kept the IVC at 20 mbar of nitrogen.

MB filling with LN

  • We raised the LHe transfer pipe a bit.
  • We opened the valve between the liquefier and the MB, and performed a counter flow from the MB to the getter.
    • When the pressure of MB was found to be oscillating, which means that the liquid nitrogen began to transfer. We controlled the pressure of MB around 890 mbar.
  • During the transfer of liquid nitrogen, we performed two capillary tests by closing v1.
  • We also stopped the counter flow of the liquefier.
  • We disconnected the liquid nitrogen transfer line.

Liquefier preparation

  • We connected the cold box to the turbo, using the “OVC” and “IVC” pipes.
  • We started pumping the cold box, by the turbo and the external pump.
  • After the pumping for one night, we turned off the turbo and stopped the coldbox pumping.
  • After the turbo is stopped, we attached the IVC to the turbo.
  • We turned on GM heads and JT, and using the cooldown bypass.
  • Periodically measure the working point.

re-transferring of liquid nitrogen

  • We inserted the liquid nitrogen transfer line and started the re-transferring of liquid nitrogen into the Dewar.
  • We put some overpressure nitrogen in the MB for re-transferring the liquid nitrogen back to the dewar.

Tests at LN temperature

  • Repeat the contact check.
  • Repeat the capillary test.
  • Repeat the leak check (if you put nitrogen as exchange gas).
    • After the transferring is complete, we use the 1KPOT pump to pump the MB.
    • We connected the IVC to the leak detector.
  • Repeat the needle valve check.
    • Re-pump MB in preparation for flow test for both precision needle valves.
  • We closed the IVC, stopped the turbo and turned off the leak detector.

MB filling with LHe

  • start to transfer without waiting for the LHe flame of the boa. The MB and the 1KPot should be in communication and both to recovery. In the IVC there should be some He gas to be used as exchange gas. Moreover, the final part of this helium transfer is different from the refill one because it has to be interrupted at precise conditions.
